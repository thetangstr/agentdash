# AgentDash Founder Sales Deck Outline

Date: 2026-04-09
Status: Working draft

Target length: 8 slides
Goal: get a qualified prospect from curiosity to pilot conversation

## Slide 1: The category and promise

Headline:

**Run AI agents in production without losing control**

Subhead:

AgentDash is the control plane for teams running AI agents in production.

What to show:

- one simple product image or mocked dashboard
- 3 short proof bullets:
  - visibility into what every agent is doing
  - approvals and human oversight
  - budget and spend control

Speaker note:

Most teams already have AI experiments. The problem is not access to models anymore. The problem is operational control.

## Slide 2: The problem

Headline:

**Most companies do not have an AI system. They have a pile.**

What to show:

- prompts in tabs
- scripts
- Zapier flows
- point tools
- scattered agents
- no owner
- no budget view

Speaker note:

This works for experiments. It breaks when AI starts doing real work every day. Nobody knows who owns what, what is in flight, what it costs, or where a human needs to step in.

## Slide 3: Why now

Headline:

**AI adoption is ahead of AI operations**

What to show:

- teams are already paying for Claude / ChatGPT
- internal AI usage is happening with or without a system
- recurring workflows are getting pushed toward AI
- trust, oversight, and cost control are becoming blockers

Speaker note:

The bottleneck is no longer "can AI do something useful?" The bottleneck is "can we run it in production in a way the business trusts?"

## Slide 4: The product

Headline:

**AgentDash is the management layer for your AI workforce**

What to show:

- agents and responsibilities
- tasks and outputs
- approvals
- heartbeats / runs
- budgets and spend
- operator dashboard

Speaker note:

We are not a chatbot and we are not just workflow glue. We are the control plane that lets one operator manage a small AI team with visibility and intervention built in.

## Slide 5: The wedge

Headline:

**Start with one workflow, not the whole company**

What to show:

- support triage
- lead research
- CRM hygiene and reporting
- content ops
- internal research

Plus one line:

2-5 agents. One team. One operator. One measurable outcome.

Speaker note:

We do not ask customers to bet the company on autonomy. We start with one high-value recurring queue, prove it works, and expand after trust is earned.

## Slide 6: The operating model

Headline:

**Human oversight is the feature**

What to show:

- approval gates
- kill switch
- budget caps
- audit trail
- BYOT / customer-controlled deployment

Speaker note:

This matters because the first objection from any serious buyer is not capability. It is governance. Oversight is what makes production rollout possible.

## Slide 7: The pilot

Headline:

**A 2-4 week pilot to prove value fast**

What to show:

- discover the workflow
- deploy 2-5 agents
- run with human review
- measure time saved, throughput, quality, and cost
- present ROI and expansion plan

Speaker note:

The pilot is meant to be a low-friction decision. We are selling a fast path to learning and proof, not a giant transformation project.

## Slide 8: The ask

Headline:

**If you already have AI sprawl, let’s pick the first workflow**

What to show:

- ideal fit bullets:
  - 20-100 person team
  - already using AI heavily
  - one recurring workflow that is painful today
  - operator champion

CTA:

- 30-minute discovery call
- identify the workflow
- decide if a pilot makes sense

Speaker note:

The right next step is not a long procurement cycle. It is a focused conversation to see whether there is a narrow workflow where AgentDash can create value quickly.

## Design guidance

Keep the deck visually sparse.
One idea per slide.
Few words, big type, one strong visual.

Avoid:

- feature matrices
- architecture diagrams
- long paragraphs
- marketplace language
- "autonomous economy" rhetoric in the main deck

## Optional appendix slides

Only add if needed for specific buyers:

2. demo workflow walkthrough
