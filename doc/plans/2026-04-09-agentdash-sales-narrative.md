# AgentDash Sales Narrative

Date: 2026-04-09
Status: Working draft

## One-line positioning

AgentDash is the control plane for teams running AI agents in production.

## Who this is for

Best fit:

- 20-100 person SaaS, agency, and services companies
- already using AI tools heavily
- feeling sprawl: prompts, scripts, Zapier, point tools, ad hoc agents
- wants automation, but with human oversight

Typical buyer:

- CEO
- COO
- Chief of Staff
- Head of Ops
- RevOps lead

## The problem in plain English

Most companies experimenting with AI do not have an AI system.
They have a pile.

They have:

- prompts in tabs
- a few scripts
- some workflows in Zapier
- maybe one or two agent tools
- no clear ownership
- no reliable approvals
- no budget visibility
- no single view of what the agents are doing

That is fine for demos. It breaks in production.

## The promise

AgentDash lets one operator run a small AI workforce in production without losing control.

With AgentDash, you can:

- define agents and responsibilities
- assign work and track outputs
- require approvals for sensitive actions
- monitor spend and enforce budget limits
- see what each agent is doing in one dashboard

## The status quo alternatives

Customers will usually compare you against one of these:

1. DIY sprawl
   Spreadsheets, prompts, scripts, Slack, Zapier, and scattered agent tools.

2. Single-purpose AI apps
   Useful for one workflow, but not a management layer for many agents.

3. Generic automation tools
   Good at routing events. Weak at agent oversight, approvals, and work tracking.

4. Big autonomous-agent demos
   Impressive in a demo. Hard to govern in a real business.

## Why customers buy

They do not buy "the autonomous economy."
They buy relief from chaos.

The real buying triggers are:

- "We already have AI doing work, but it's messy."
- "We need a human in the loop."
- "We need to know what it's costing."
- "We want to start with one team and expand safely."

## The wedge

Do not sell "AI for the whole company" first.

Sell:

> We help you stand up your first 2-5 production AI agents around one high-value workflow.

Best first workflows:

- support triage and draft responses
- lead research and qualification
- CRM hygiene and weekly reporting
- content operations and first drafts
- internal research and recurring reporting

These are good because they are repeatable, measurable, and easy to review.

## 30-second talk track

Most companies using AI today have a mess of prompts, scripts, and point tools. That works until you want agents doing real work every day. Then the question becomes: who is doing what, what does it cost, and where does a human need to approve? AgentDash is the control plane for that. We help you run a small AI workforce in production with visibility, approvals, budget controls, and clear work tracking, starting with one team and one workflow.

## 2-minute talk track

What we see in the market is that teams do not actually have an AI operating model yet. They have scattered experiments: a few people using Claude, maybe some automations, maybe one or two agents, but no real control layer around them.

That becomes a problem the minute AI starts touching important work. Somebody needs to know what each agent is responsible for, what work is in flight, what outputs were produced, what the spend looks like, and which actions need human approval.

That is what AgentDash does. It is the control plane for running AI agents in production. You can define agents, assign work, review outputs, gate sensitive actions, track spend, and intervene when needed.

We do not ask customers to automate the whole company on day 1. We start with one high-value workflow and 2-5 agents, prove value in a couple of weeks, and then expand from there once the operating model is trusted.

## Discovery questions

Use these on calls:

1. Where is AI already being used in the company today, even informally?
2. What work is repetitive enough that you would trust AI to do 80 percent of it with review?
3. Where do things fall through the cracks today because nobody owns the follow-up?
4. If you deployed 3 agents next month, what would you want them doing first?
5. What would make you nervous about letting agents touch production work?
6. If this worked, how would you measure value in 30 days?

## Objection handling

### "We already have ChatGPT / Claude."

That is not the same category. Those are model interfaces. AgentDash is the management layer for ongoing agent work across a team.

### "Why not just use Zapier?"

Zapier is good for event plumbing. It is not a control plane for multiple agents with budgets, approvals, and operator visibility.

### "We do not want fully autonomous agents."

Good. Neither do we on day 1. The product is built around oversight, intervention, and gradual expansion.

### "This sounds like consulting."

The onboarding is hands-on because that is the fastest way to get to value. The product is still the thing being sold: the persistent control plane the customer keeps using after the pilot.

## What not to say on early calls

- "We are building the backbone of the autonomous economy."
- "You can replace whole departments."
- "It works for every team out of the box."
- "Think of it like employees, but AI."

Those lines either sound inflated or trigger fear faster than trust.

## Closing line

The simple pitch is:

> You already have AI experiments. We help you turn them into a production system you can actually manage.
