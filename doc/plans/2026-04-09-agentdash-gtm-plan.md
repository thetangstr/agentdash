# AgentDash GTM Plan

Date: 2026-04-09
Status: Working plan

## Objective

Land the first 3-5 paying customers by selling AgentDash as the control plane for teams running AI agents in production, then expand each account from one workflow to multiple departments.

This is a founder-led, services-assisted GTM motion.
Do not optimize for broad awareness yet.
Optimize for fast learning, trust, and repeatable proof of value.

## What we are selling

Primary product:

- AgentDash as the control plane for production AI agents

Primary outcome:

- one operator can run 2-5 AI agents on a real workflow with visibility, approvals, budget controls, and clear task/output tracking

Initial commercial offer:

- 2-4 week pilot
- one high-value workflow
- 2-5 agents
- weekly review
- ROI report and expansion plan at the end

## Positioning

External positioning:

> AgentDash is the control plane for teams running AI agents in production.

Promise:

- turn scattered AI experiments into a managed operating system
- keep humans in control
- start with one team and expand only after proving ROI

Do not lead with:

- autonomous company rhetoric
- giant feature lists
- CRM as the main category
- full-company replacement language

## ICP

### Tier 1 ICP

- 20-100 person SaaS companies
- already paying for Claude, ChatGPT, or similar
- operational pain around support, revops, internal reporting, content, or research
- buyer is close to execution and willing to sponsor a pilot quickly

### Tier 2 ICP

- agencies and professional services firms
- AI-enabled internal teams with recurring client workflows
- strong need for oversight and auditability

### Not now

- very large enterprises with heavy procurement and security review
- teams that only want a chatbot
- companies with no existing AI behavior
- buyers asking for fully autonomous operation on day 1

## Beachhead use cases

Choose only one per account for the pilot.

Best first use cases:

- support ticket triage and response drafting
- inbound lead research and qualification
- CRM hygiene and recurring reporting
- content operations and first-draft generation
- internal research and weekly executive reporting

Selection criteria:

- recurring volume
- measurable output
- clear review loop
- painful today
- low integration risk

## Offer design

### Pilot

Goal:

- prove one workflow can be run by 2-5 agents with operator oversight

Structure:

- week 0 discovery and workflow selection
- week 1 deployment and agent setup
- week 2-3 operation and iteration
- week 4 ROI review and expansion proposal

Commercials:

- keep current pilot pricing simple
- minimize procurement friction
- fixed scope
- explicit success metrics before kickoff

### Production expansion

After pilot success, expand in this order:

1. more volume in same workflow
2. adjacent workflow in same department
3. second department
4. broader rollout with standard operating cadence

## Core GTM motion

### Motion

1. identify a buyer with visible AI sprawl
2. run discovery to find the highest-value narrow workflow
3. sell the low-friction pilot
4. deliver measurable value in 2-4 weeks
5. convert to annual platform + support
6. expand department by department

### Why this motion fits the product

The product is governance-heavy and workflow-specific.
That means trust matters more than top-of-funnel volume.
You win by showing that agents can do useful work without becoming a black box.

## Channels

Focus on channels that produce high-context conversations.

### 1. Founder network and warm intros

Best first channel.
The product still needs trust and explanation.

Targets:

- founders
- operator-heavy SMB leaders
- heads of ops / revops
- friends-of-friends already experimenting with AI internally

### 2. Design-partner outbound

Build a list of 50-100 companies that fit the ICP.
Send highly tailored outreach based on a specific workflow.

Good trigger signals:

- hiring for revops, support ops, content ops
- public evidence of AI enthusiasm
- small team carrying too much operational load
- fast-growing company with recurring internal queues

### 3. Content that sells the problem, not the platform

Write short founder-led posts around:

- "your AI stack is a pile, not a system"
- "how to run AI agents in production without losing control"
- "what a board operator for AI agents actually does"

Do not write vague AI-thought-leadership content.
Write operator content that creates qualified replies.

### 4. Live demos

Use a prepared demo environment tied to a concrete workflow.
Do not demo every module.
Show one operator managing one AI team.

## Messaging by audience

### CEO / Founder

- your team already has AI experiments
- AgentDash turns that into a system you can actually manage
- start with one workflow, prove value, then expand

### COO / Chief of Staff / Ops

- get visibility into what agents are doing
- add approvals and intervention points
- stop tasks from disappearing into prompt chaos

### RevOps / Functional lead

- use agents on a recurring queue without losing auditability
- measure throughput, quality, and cost
- keep a human review loop where it matters

### IT / Security

- self-hosted or customer-controlled deployment
- BYOT
- audit trail, budget limits, approval gates, kill switch

## Funnel design

Do not overbuild a funnel yet.
Use a simple pipeline:

1. target account identified
2. discovery call booked
3. qualified workflow identified
4. pilot proposal sent
5. pilot launched
6. pilot completed
7. converted to paid production
8. expanded account

## First 90 days

### Days 1-30

Goal:

- tighten messaging
- create target list
- run discovery calls

Actions:

- finalize homepage and deck around the new positioning
- build a 50-account ICP list
- write outreach variants for 3 workflows
- book 10-15 discovery calls
- run every call personally
- capture objections, vocabulary, and buying triggers

Output:

- one repeatable call narrative
- one sharp pilot proposal template
- one demo per beachhead workflow

### Days 31-60

Goal:

- close first pilots

Actions:

- convert strongest discovery accounts into 2-4 pilots
- keep pilot scope narrow
- run weekly reviews with buyers
- collect before/after metrics and customer quotes

Output:

- 2-4 live design partners
- 2 strong case studies in progress
- clearer ICP fit signal

### Days 61-90

Goal:

- convert pilots and establish expansion playbook

Actions:

- present ROI reviews
- close annual production agreements
- expand successful pilots into second workflows
- systematize onboarding and reporting

Output:

- first 3-5 paying accounts
- first expansion motion
- first case-study-backed outbound loop

## Metrics

Track only a few metrics at first:

- discovery calls booked
- qualified opportunities
- pilots proposed
- pilots launched
- pilot-to-production conversion
- average days from first call to pilot start
- average days from pilot start to paid conversion
- expansion rate by account
- ROI metrics from pilot workflows

## Success criteria

This GTM plan is working if, within 90 days:

- at least 10 good discovery conversations happen
- at least 2 pilots launch
- at least 1 pilot converts to paid production
- the same workflow pattern appears across multiple accounts
- buyers repeat back the positioning in their own words

## Risks

### Risk 1. Positioning stays too broad

If the pitch sounds like a platform catalog, buyers will not know where to start.

Mitigation:

- always pitch one use case first
- keep the control-plane framing primary

### Risk 2. Pilots become custom services projects

If every pilot needs bespoke engineering, GTM will stall.

Mitigation:

- restrict pilots to repeatable workflow patterns
- keep integration scope tight

### Risk 3. Buyers want autonomy faster than trust allows

Some prospects will want "fully autonomous" demos.
That can create false expectations.

Mitigation:

- sell oversight as the feature, not the concession

### Risk 4. Founder gets stuck in delivery and stops learning

If execution consumes all time, messaging and pipeline never sharpen.

Mitigation:

- reserve weekly time for outbound, calls, and synthesis
- document patterns after every pilot

## Hard recommendation

For the next phase, run AgentDash like this:

- founder-led sales
- narrow workflow pilots
- self-hosted / BYOT / governance-first message
- expansion through proven operational value

This is not a mass-market SaaS launch yet.
It is a disciplined land-and-expand motion to find the repeatable wedge.
